Atif Aslam

March 12, 1983

The Legend Of Pakistani Music Industry

Atif Aslam (Urdu: عاطف اسلم, born 12 March 1983) is a Pakistani playback singer, songwriter, composer, and actor. He has recorded many songs in Pakistan and India and is known for his vocal belting, technique. Aslam predominantly sings in Urdu but also in Hindi, Punjabi, Bengali, and Pashto. In 2008, he received the Tamgha-e-Imtiaz, the fourth-highest civilian honour award from the Pakistani government. He is also a recipient of several Lux Style Awards. In 2019, he was awarded a star on the Dubai Walk of Fame after his nomination for the best singer in Pakistan. He was also featured in Forbes Asia's 100 Digital Stars, published in December 2020.

Biography

  • Atif Aslam is a Pakistani singer, songwriter, and actor who was born on March 12, 1983, in Wazirabad, Pakistan.
  • He started his music career as the lead vocalist of the Pakistani rock band, Jal, in 2003, but he left the band in 2004 to pursue a solo career.
  • Atif Aslam made his debut in the Indian film industry as a playback singer in the 2005 Bollywood film, Zeher, and his song, "Woh Lamhe," became an instant hit.
  • Over the years, Atif Aslam has become one of the most popular and successful singers in Pakistan and India, with hit songs such as "Tere Bin," "Pehli Nazar Mein," and "Tajdar-e-Haram."
  • In addition to his music career, Atif Aslam has also acted in a few Pakistani films, including "Bol" and "Actor in Law." He is known for his philanthropic work and has supported various charities over the years.
